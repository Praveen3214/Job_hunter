### 2.4 How to Approach Executive Search Firms

> **Critical insight:** You are not the client -- the hiring company is. Search firms work for the employer. Your goal is to be in their database and top-of-mind.

**Step-by-step approach:**

1. **Register your profile** on the firm's website (Korn Ferry, Michael Page, etc. all have candidate portals)
2. **Build relationships with partners** -- identify the partner who leads the marketing/consumer practice via LinkedIn
3. **Send a concise intro email** (not your resume): 2-3 sentences on your current role, what you are looking for, and one notable achievement
4. **Get referrals** -- a warm introduction from a CEO or board member they have placed is gold
5. **Stay visible** -- share thought leadership content on LinkedIn that the consultant can see
6. **Be responsive** -- when a headhunter calls, always take the call, even if the role is not right. Relationships compound.
7. **Be honest about compensation** -- they will benchmark you anyway. Inflating numbers burns bridges.

## 3. Networking & Direct Outreach

### 3.1 LinkedIn Strategies for Senior Marketing Roles

LinkedIn is where 80% of senior marketing hiring decisions start or end. Here is a tactical playbook:

**Profile Optimization:**
- Headline: Not just your title. Use format: `VP Marketing | Built [X brand] from 0 to [Y revenue] | [Industry] + [Specialty]`
- About section: Write in first person. Lead with a quantified achievement. End with what you are looking for.
- Featured section: Pin 2-3 posts showcasing marketing results, campaign case studies, or thought leadership
- Recommendations: Get 3+ from CEOs/founders you have reported to

**Content Strategy (The 30-Day Visibility Sprint):**
- Post 3x/week: 1 career insight, 1 marketing trend take, 1 case study / war story
- Comment meaningfully on posts from CEOs, VCs, and CMOs at your target companies
- Write 1 long-form article/month on a marketing trend (LinkedIn Newsletter is even better)
- Target: Within 30 days, headhunters start finding you instead of you finding them

**Direct Outreach Playbook:**
- Identify 20 target companies. Find the CEO/founder and the CHRO on LinkedIn.
- Send a connection request with a **specific, non-generic note**: reference a recent company announcement + how your experience is relevant
- Follow up with a value-add message (share a relevant article, insight, or industry benchmark)
- Do NOT lead with "I'm looking for a job." Lead with value.

### 3.4 Referral Strategies

- **The 5-5-5 method:** Identify 5 ex-colleagues who are now C-suite, 5 board members you know, and 5 VCs/PE partners. Have a focused conversation with each. Not "do you know of any openings" but "who are the most interesting companies in [sector] right now?"
- **Reverse referrals:** Help others first. Make 2-3 introductions for other people per week. Reciprocity is real at the executive level.
- **Board/advisory route:** Take 1-2 advisory board seats at startups. This is often the pipeline to a full-time CMO role.
- **VC/PE network:** Marketing leaders are frequently sourced through VC portfolio networks. Build relationships with operating partners at Sequoia, Accel, Matrix, Peak XV, Elevation, etc.

### 3.5 Personal Branding for Marketing Executives

If you are a marketing leader who cannot market themselves, that is a red flag. Your personal brand IS your resume at this level.

- **Speak at events:** Even small events count. 2-3 speaking gigs/year builds visibility.
- **Publish:** Write for exchange4media, Campaign India, LinkedIn, or start a Substack.
- **Mentor:** Become a mentor on ADPList or similar platforms. Visibility + goodwill.
- **Awards:** Submit campaigns you have led for industry awards (Effies, Abbys, etc.)
- **Podcast appearances:** Reach out to marketing podcasts. Many actively seek CMO guests.

## 4. What Approach Works Best

### 4.1 How Senior Roles Are Actually Filled

| Channel | % of Senior/CXO Placements | Notes |
|---------|---------------------------|-------|
| **Networking / referrals** | ~40-50% | The dominant channel for C-suite. Warm introductions to boards and CEOs. |
| **Executive search firms (headhunters)** | ~30-35% | Retained search for most VP+ roles at large companies. |
| **Direct sourcing (LinkedIn)** | ~10-15% | Companies increasingly have in-house executive recruiters mining LinkedIn. |
| **Job boards / portals** | ~5-10% | Mostly relevant for Director-level. Rare for true C-suite unless at startups. |
| **Internal promotions** | Variable | Many CMO roles are filled internally. Be aware of this when targeting. |

> **Key takeaway:** At the VP/CMO level, the visible job market (portals, boards) represents only the tip of the iceberg. The vast majority of roles are filled through relationships and search firms. Your strategy must be weighted accordingly.

### 4.2 What Makes Marketing Job Search Different

- **Portfolio matters more than resume:** Be prepared with a "greatest hits" deck -- 3-5 campaigns/initiatives with business results (revenue, growth %, brand metrics).
- **Cultural fit is weighted heavily:** Marketing leaders set the tone for brand voice and company culture. Expect extensive culture-fit interviews.
- **T-shaped expectations:** Companies want breadth (brand, digital, performance, product marketing) AND depth in at least 1-2 areas.
- **Board/investor scrutiny:** At the CMO level, expect to meet the board or investors during the hiring process.
- **Revenue accountability:** The trend is heavily toward marketing leaders who can tie activity to revenue. Be prepared to discuss attribution, CAC, LTV, and revenue marketing.
- **Interim/fractional is growing:** Many companies now hire fractional CMOs before committing to full-time. Consider this as a foot-in-the-door strategy.

### 4.3 Typical Timeline for Senior Marketing Role Searches

| Stage | Duration | What Happens |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Market scanning + networking activation | Weeks 1-4 | Register with search firms, optimize LinkedIn, activate network |
| Active conversations begin | Weeks 4-8 | First meetings with headhunters, exploratory conversations |
| Interview processes | Weeks 8-16 | Multi-round interviews (4-6 rounds typical for CMO roles) |
| Offer negotiation + notice period | Weeks 16-24 | Negotiation can take 2-4 weeks alone. Senior notice periods in India are often 60-90 days. |
| **Total realistic timeline** | **4-6 months** | For the right fit. Can be shorter at startups (2-3 months). |

> Patience is non-negotiable. Rushing into a wrong-fit CMO role is a career-damaging move. The average CMO tenure is ~3.5 years (Spencer Stuart). Choose wisely.

### 4.4 Red Flags & Common Mistakes

**Red flags in companies:**
- No clear reporting line (reporting to both CEO and COO is a recipe for failure)
- "We need someone to do everything" -- usually means no budget and unrealistic expectations
- High CMO turnover (3 CMOs in 5 years = structural problem)
- Marketing team is entirely outsourced / agency-dependent with no in-house ambition
- No seat at the leadership table (marketing reports to sales or ops)

**Common mistakes by candidates:**
- Applying to too many roles on portals instead of investing in relationships
- Underselling business impact (talking about campaigns instead of business outcomes)
- Ignoring personal brand ("the cobbler's children have no shoes" problem)
- Being inflexible on industry (a great B2C marketing leader can often transition to D2C)
- Negotiating only on base salary and ignoring ESOPs, RSUs, performance bonuses
- Not doing deep enough diligence on the hiring company's actual marketing maturity
